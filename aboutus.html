What’s greening out?

Greening out is a term used to describe the uncomfortable set of symptoms that can occur after consuming an amount of cannabis that exceeds an individual’s tolerance threshold. In other words, it’s when you consume more than your body can handle.

This phenomenon can happen to both novice and seasoned users, and although it can come about after mixing cannabis with alcohol, you can green out simply by consuming marijuana on an empty stomach.

So, if you feel you’re greening out or you notice one of your friends is starting to fade, knowing what’s going on is key since it will help you deal with the issue and help you feel more in control of the situation.

What’s causes greening out?

Greening out is primarily the result of consuming more cannabis than one’s body can comfortably handle. Several factors contribute to this phenomenon:

  • THC: This is the main psychoactive compound in cannabis. The stronger it is, the more it can overload your level of tolerance.
  • Consumption methods: Different methods of consuming cannabis can result in varying intensities of its effects.
  • Dehydration and hunger: Being dehydrated or having an empty stomach might amplify the effects of THC or contribute to feelings of dizziness and nausea.
  • Tolerance: The amount your body can handle, which varies from person to person. someone with a low tolerance, like a new consumer or someone who uses cannabis infrequently, is more susceptible to greening out from smaller amounts.
  • Mixing with other substances: Combining cannabis with alcohol, prescription medications, or other drugs can amplify its effects or lead to unpredictable reactions. It can be dangerous to mix different substances. Don’t do this!

Greening out symptoms

The experience of greening out can vary from person to person, but some common symptoms and sensations include:

  • Nausea and Vominting: A common reaction to overconsumption.
  • Dizziness:Feeling lightheaded or off-balance.
  • Anxiety and Paranoia: Heightened feelings of unease or fear.
  • Sweating:Including cold sweats
  • Heart Palpitations: An uncomfortably noticeable heartbeat.
